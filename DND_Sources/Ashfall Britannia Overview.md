Ashfall Britannia Overview

You were children when the sky broke.

Some of you remember it clearly—others only in flashes—but every soul alive recalls the Night of Ashfall.

You were no older than ten when the heavens roared. A burning star, brighter than a thousand torches, split the clouds and screamed across the world. Windows shattered. Animals panicked. The earth itself trembled beneath your feet.

And then… the ash came.

Not snow. Not soot.
Something heavier. Older. Wrong.

It fell in thick waves, coating rooftops, fields, and every open hand held up in fear or wonder. The sky dimmed. The sun weakened. Days became gray, cold, and short.

The grown-ups tried to calm you.

“It will pass.”
“It’s just dust.”
“The light will return.”

They were wrong.

Because in the weeks after the meteor’s fall, the whispers began—stories of pale figures moving through the fog. Of travelers vanishing on the roads. Of something watching from the treeline at dusk.

And then the first village went silent.

You all remember the moment you learned the truth:

The vampires had returned.

Some of you lost family that night.
Some fled burning homes.
Some were rescued by soldiers at the last possible moment.
But all of you saw something—a shadow… a shape… or eyes glinting red through the drifting ash—that you never forgot.

Years passed. The castle-cities rose. Walls were built. Gates sealed. Humanity learned to live behind steel and steam, praying the ash would lift.

But it never did.

And now, the children who survived the Night of Ashfall have grown.

You stand in the courtyard of Greyhaven Keep, gaslamps flickering in the dim morning haze. The ash falls the same way it did all those years ago—soft, silent, unending.

You are no longer helpless children.

You are recruits of The Ashwardens—
the ones who venture beyond the walls,
the ones who face the things that once hunted you,
the ones who refuse to hide any longer.

The iron gates groan open. Cold wind sweeps in. Somewhere beyond the wall, something howls through the fog.

Your childhood ended the night the sky fell.
Your true life begins now.

Welcome, Ashwardens.
The ash remembers you.
The darkness does too.

🌍 World Lore (from #lore, posted 4/9/26 by SickOpossum & Barrett/Doug)
The History of Britannia:
Chapter 1 — The Gift of Dawn: In a golden age long past, the Sun Goddess Aurona (She Who Rises) descended on the longest solstice day to the cliffs of Highreach and gifted the people of Britannia the Sun Shard — a fragment of concentrated daylight. Under its power, Britannia flourished for a hundred years: perfect harvests, softened winters, retreating plagues, and a golden age of art and trade. Solar priests channeled the Shard's warmth. But enemies who could not abide the sun had already taken notice.
Chapter 1 — Origins of the Vampire Rule: In the "Withering Century" — a time of famine and plague — thirteen desperate mortals (scholars, warlords, healers, heretics) sought power in the ruins beneath Hollow Dawn Keep. They bargained with an ancient night-spirit and were transformed into the first vampires: The Thirteen of the Hollow Dawn. They gained immortality, superhuman strength, and mastery over life-essence, but were cursed with undying hunger and eternal night. Their blood created lesser vampires — soldiers and courtiers. A fractured, starving world surrendered to them, and the Vampire Rule began.

⚔️ Notable Items (from #lore)
The Twin Goddesses' Daggers — Emberkiss & Frostfall: Forged in Britannia's dawn years as gifts from the Sister Goddesses of Fire and Ice, Astraea Ignis and Astraea Glacia, to arm a mortal champion capable of restoring balance.

Emberkiss (Fire Dagger): Born from a shard of the first star; burns with an ember-glow, and wounds smolder with glowing ash.
Frostfall (Ice Dagger): Carved from an ancient glacier; pale blue runes breathe mist, and cuts spread numbing cold.
Twin-Bound Nature: When wielded together, they stabilize volatile magic. Separated, each grows more extreme and dangerous.

In short: Ashfall Britannia is a dark fantasy DnD campaign set in a post-apocalyptic Britannia shrouded in supernatural ash, where vampires rule the night, humanity huddles in walled castle-cities, and the player characters are newly recruited Ashwardens tasked with venturing beyond the walls to fight back.
