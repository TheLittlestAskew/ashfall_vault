Bishop Alistair, 

I write to you with growing concern. For the past three nights, several of us have heard unfamiliar voices and footsteps outside our homes well past midnight. Lantern‑light has been seen moving along the old road, though no travelers ever arrive by morning. Some claim to have glimpsed figures standing at the tree line, watching, then vanishing when approached. We cannot say whether this is simple mischief or something more troubling, but the pattern is clear and unsettling. I ask that you send guidance—or, if you deem it necessary, someone from the church to investigate before matters worsen. Your faithful servant, our Eminence, I beg your attention on a matter I approach with great reluctance. Our bishop, whom we are meant to trust as shepherd of this diocese, has shown troubling signs of corruption. In recent months he has diverted tithes without explanation, granted favors to those who flatter him, and dismissed any who question his decisions. Several clergy have quietly expressed fear of speaking openly. I do not make this claim lightly. The welfare of our people and the integrity of the Church compel me to bring this to you. I ask that you look into these concerns and determine whether an inquiry is warranted before further harm is done. 

In humility and sincerity,
