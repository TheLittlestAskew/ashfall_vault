
Among vampire society, few figures are as misunderstood as the [Beast Masters]. While most vampires cultivate supernatural strength, speed, and martial prowess, Beast Masters develop a very different gift. Their bodies remain thin, fragile, and physically unimpressive even by mortal standards. In direct combat, a Beast Master is often at a severe disadvantage against nearly any other vampire. Yet this apparent weakness hides a terrifying power. The blood of a Beast Master carries a rare psychic mutation that allows them to establish mental bonds with creatures known as Vampire Beast Thralls. These monstrous servants are created through forbidden rituals involving corrupted blood, animal instincts, and vampiric essence. Once bound, a thrall becomes an extension of the Beast Master's will. 
A Beast Master can see through a thrall's eyes, hear through its ears, and communicate commands instantly across great distances. Skilled Beast Masters can coordinate entire packs as if they were a single organism, directing ambushes, scouting operations, and assaults with frightening precision. Because of their frailty, Beast Masters rarely appear on battlefields personally. Instead, they remain hidden within heavily guarded lairs, abandoned crypts, or fortified sanctuaries while their thralls carry out their commands. Killing a Beast Master is often far more difficult than defeating one, as enemies must first survive the hordes of creatures standing between them and their true target. Within vampire politics, Beast Masters occupy a strange position. They are seldom respected for physical might, but they are feared for their influence. A lone warrior vampire may dominate a duel, but a Beast Master commands an army. Many vampire lords employ them as scouts, wardens, beast tamers, or battlefield strategists. The psychic bond between master and thrall is not without risk. Every creature linked to the Beast Master leaves traces of its instincts within their mind. Over decades, many Beast Masters become increasingly detached from human thought, adopting predatory behaviors, animalistic mannerisms, and an unsettling pack mentality. Some eventually lose the distinction between their own identity and that of their countless thralls. 
Among vampire legends, there are tales of ancient Beast Masters who controlled entire swarms of monstrous creatures, turning forests into living hunting grounds and transforming battlefields into coordinated feeding frenzies. 

Whether these stories are true remains unknown. 
What is certain is this:
- A Beast Master standing alone is vulnerable. A Beast Master with their thralls is never truly alone.
